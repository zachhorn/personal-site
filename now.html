  3. Working on the idea that an arbitrage is created at the moment someone decides to sell a piece of furniture. The arbitrage occurs between the price a person would sell that piece of furniture, and the price a person would pay for that same piece of furniture. Delivery and pickup are value added services. You would be offering liquidity in a relatively illiquid market. I have first-hand experience with this market: People will throw away premium furniture (i.e. $3,500 - $7,500 sofas, moderate use), because they don't want to go through the hassle of selling.

    Phases

    1. Bootstrap concept: Buy and sell furniture in existing marketplaces.
    2. Use profits from Phase 1 to develop the platform for seamless buying and selling.
    3. Scale inventory.
